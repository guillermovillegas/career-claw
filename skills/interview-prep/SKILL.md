## Company Research Workflow

Run this checklist before every interview:

1. **Company website** -- product, pricing, team page, recent blog posts
2. **Recent news** -- Google "[Company name] news" for last 90 days (funding, launches, partnerships)
3. **Glassdoor** -- interview reviews for the specific role or team, salary data, culture signals
4. **LinkedIn** -- interviewer profiles, mutual connections, company headcount trends
5. **Product** -- sign up for a free trial or demo if available; note UX observations
6. **Competitors** -- identify 2-3 direct competitors and how the company differentiates
7. **Tech stack** -- check job posting, StackShare, GitHub repos for technology choices

Summarize findings in a brief prep doc with: company overview, recent developments, interviewer backgrounds, product observations, and 2-3 thoughtful questions to ask.

## STAR Story Framework

Use the STAR method for behavioral questions:

- **Situation:** Set the scene (company, context, challenge)
- **Task:** Your specific responsibility
- **Action:** What you did (focus on YOUR actions, not the team's)
- **Result:** Quantifiable outcome with metrics

Delivery tips:

- Keep each story to 60-90 seconds
- Lead with the result if the story is long
- Have 5-6 stories ready that cover different competencies
- Practice transitions: "That reminds me of..." or "A good example of that is..."

## Day-of Checklist

1. Review prep doc and interviewer backgrounds (30 min before)
2. Have 3 thoughtful questions ready for the interviewer
3. Open portfolio site and relevant project demos
4. Test video/audio setup 10 minutes before
5. Keep talk tracks and STAR stories accessible
6. Have a notepad ready for names, questions, and follow-up items
7. Send thank-you email within 2-4 hours (use outreach skill)
