# Follow-up Sequences

Detailed multi-step follow-up cadences with timing, tone guidance, and example text for each step.

---

## 1. Job Application Follow-up (3-step)

Use after submitting an application through a company portal or recruiter.

### Step 1: Day 3 -- Light Touch

**Tone:** Friendly, brief, adds new value. Not pushy.

**Subject:** Following up on [Role] application at [Company]

Hi [Name],

I submitted my application for [Role] a few days ago and wanted to make sure it landed on your radar. Since applying, [insert new value: "I also saw your team's recent blog post on X -- really aligned with work I've done at Levee" or "I just wrapped up a project using [relevant tech] that's directly applicable"].

I'd welcome the chance to discuss how my background in [key area] could support what you're building.

Best,
Guillermo

---

### Step 2: Day 7 -- Restate Fit

**Tone:** Respectful of their time. Concise restatement of why you fit. Offer to help them say yes or no.

**Subject:** Re: Following up on [Role] application at [Company]

Hi [Name],

I know your inbox is full -- just a brief follow-up on my application for [Role]. My experience leading product and engineering at Levee (60% inspection time reduction, award-winning AI platform) and managing a $250M+ portfolio at Chamberlain maps closely to what you're looking for.

Happy to provide any additional context, references, or work samples. Also totally understand if the timing doesn't work.

Best,
Guillermo

---

### Step 3: Day 14 -- Graceful Close

**Tone:** Zero pressure. Leave door open. Short.

**Subject:** Re: Following up on [Role] application at [Company]

Hi [Name],

Checking in one last time on the [Role] position. If the role has been filled or the timing isn't right, completely understand.

I'd be glad to stay in touch for future opportunities. Wishing you and the team the best.

Best,
Guillermo

---

## 2. Freelance Proposal Follow-up (2-step)

Use after sending a proposal, estimate, or project pitch to a potential client.

### Step 1: Day 3 -- Add Context

**Tone:** Helpful, not salesy. Share something that reduces their risk.

**Subject:** Re: [Project/Proposal name]

Hi [Name],

Following up on the proposal I sent over. Happy to answer any questions or adjust scope based on your priorities.

One thing I forgot to mention -- I built a similar [type of system] for [client/project] that [specific result]. I can share a quick demo if that would be helpful in evaluating the approach.

Best,
Guillermo

---

### Step 2: Day 7 -- Final Check

**Tone:** Brief, professional, no desperation.

**Subject:** Re: [Project/Proposal name]

Hi [Name],

Wanted to check in on the proposal. If you've decided to go a different direction, no worries at all -- appreciate you considering me.

If you'd like to revisit this later or have a different project in mind, I'm always reachable.

Best,
Guillermo

---

## 3. Networking Follow-up (2-step)

Use after meeting someone at an event, on LinkedIn, or through a mutual connection.

### Step 1: Day 1-2 -- Same-day or Next-day

**Tone:** Warm, reference something specific from the conversation. Brief.

**Subject:** Good connecting - [reference to conversation topic]

Hi [Name],

Great meeting you at [event] / Great chatting on [platform]. I really liked your point about [specific thing they said].

I'd enjoy continuing the conversation. If you're ever up for a coffee or quick call, I'm easy to reach.

Best,
Guillermo

---

### Step 2: Day 14 -- Keep Warm

**Tone:** Low-key, share something valuable rather than just "checking in."

**Subject:** Thought you'd find this interesting

Hi [Name],

Came across [article, tool, event, idea] and thought of our conversation about [topic]. [One sentence on why it's relevant.]

Hope things are going well at [Company]. Always happy to trade notes.

Best,
Guillermo

---

## 4. Post-Interview Follow-up (1 thank-you + 1 check-in)

### Step 1: Same Day -- Thank You

Send within 2-4 hours of the interview ending. See the Thank You template in email-templates.md.

**Key rules:**

- Reference a specific topic from the conversation (proves you were engaged)
- Keep it under 100 words
- Don't rehash your entire background
- Mention the next step they described

---

### Step 2: Day 5 (if no response on next steps) -- Gentle Check-in

**Tone:** Patient, brief. Not anxious.

**Subject:** Re: [Original thank-you subject]

Hi [Name],

Just wanted to check in on next steps for the [Role] process. I'm flexible on timing and happy to provide any additional information your team needs.

Looking forward to hearing from you.

Best,
Guillermo

## General Timing Rules

| Sequence           | Step 1   | Step 2 | Step 3 |
| ------------------ | -------- | ------ | ------ |
| Job Application    | Day 3    | Day 7  | Day 14 |
| Freelance Proposal | Day 3    | Day 7  | --     |
| Networking         | Day 1-2  | Day 14 | --     |
| Post-Interview     | Same day | Day 5  | --     |

**Stop conditions:**

- Any response (positive or negative) resets the sequence -- respond naturally
- If the role is confirmed filled, send a brief "thanks, stay in touch" and close
- Never send more than 3 follow-ups on the same thread without a response
- If they ask you to stop or indicate disinterest, stop immediately
